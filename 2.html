The European Union is a group of 27 European countries.
After World War II, six countries decided to team up to jointly control their industrial production.

This meant that none of them would be able to make weapons on its own and start a new war. They increased their cooperation in 1957 by creating the ‘European Economic Community’ (EEC), which was the forerunner of today’s European Union (EU).

When travelling between most EU countries, you no longer have to show your passport. 20 EU countries use the same currency: the euro.The EU has three common symbols: a flag (12 golden stars on a blue background) a hymn (Beethoven's 'Ode to Joy')

a motto ('United in diversity') Are you ready to discover what is special about each EU country?
